### Topic 2: <p style="text-align: center;">Ye Shall Prosper From the Land: Three Financial Lessons From the Bible</p>

**Speaker:** MARK SKOUSEN, Ph.D.

**Title:** Presidential Fellow, Chapman University; Editor in Chief, Forecasts &amp; Strategies

In 2014, Skousen was appointed as a presidential fellow at Chapman University. In 2004–05, he taught economics and finance at Columbia Business School and Columbia University. He was a columnist for Forbes magazine.

**Key Learning Points:**

- The significance of the Abrahamic Covenant now: “If you keep my commandments, you should prosper in the land.” Why Jewish people don’t feel guilty about money and sex, and Christians do

- Mark Weber’s “The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism” and how John Wesley’s “Sermon on Wealth” in 1743 made the West rich

- How to apply Benjamin Franklin’s trinity of virtues (industry, thrift, prudence) to investing today
